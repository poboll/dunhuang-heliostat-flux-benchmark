# Reviewer Risk and Response Memo

This memo is for internal revision before submission. It should not be uploaded as-is unless converted into a response letter after review.

## Risk 1: The layout may be seen as artificial

Likely reviewer concern: The field is generated by an algorithm rather than confirmed plant survey coordinates.

Current response: The manuscript does not claim the generated candidates are the actual plant layout. It preserves the available 11,915-coordinate pool, discloses the 20-heliostat gap relative to the reported 11,935 mirrors, and applies sector, annular, spacing, axis-ratio, terrain, and visual-feasibility gates. The contribution is a benchmark and candidate generator, not a civil-design drawing.

Needed strengthening: Keep the layout-realism figure prominent and avoid any wording that implies "true Dunhuang field".

## Risk 2: Reduced SolTrace may be considered insufficient

Likely reviewer concern: The direct ray-tracing layer uses sampled heliostats and representative solar conditions.

Current response: The paper explicitly calls this a reduced direct check, not full experimental validation. It is still useful because it tests custom aimpoints directly, uses all-phase reduced PySolTrace direct-ray construction with verified reflector-to-receiver stage ordering, reports receiver intercept ratios below unity, and shows that optical gain, proxy aiming, and direct receiver-risk checks do not collapse to a single universal layout. This is a stronger and more honest result than claiming a final annual receiver-flux solution.

Needed strengthening: Do not reintroduce any pre-correction or superseded 270-case SolTrace numbers as headline results. Keep the all-phase matrix, stage-order sanity check, and remaining full-field verification need visible.

## Risk 3: The paper may look like a stitched workflow

Likely reviewer concern: Layout generation, SolarPILOT numerical checking, aiming proxy, and SolTrace tests may appear loosely connected.

Current response: The story must stay centered on a single question: how to construct a reproducible Dunhuang-scale full-field candidate queue without confusing optical screening with receiver-risk numerical verification. Every section should map to that queue.

Needed strengthening: In the introduction and discussion, repeatedly separate four evidence layers: geometry feasibility, SolarPILOT default aiming, proxy aiming, and reduced direct ray tracing.

## Risk 4: The result is not positive enough

Likely reviewer concern: No candidate is a clear winner across all metrics.

Current response: That is the paper's most defensible scientific result. A single optical winner would be weak because it could hide receiver-flux risk. The mixed result demonstrates why benchmarked numerical-checking queues matter.

Needed strengthening: Avoid apologetic wording. Describe disagreement as a finding, not a failure.

## Risk 5: SCI Q2 ambition

Current assessment: The revised direction is much stronger than a data-descriptor framing, but *Solar Energy* remains a serious target. Acceptance will likely depend on whether reviewers view the reduced SolTrace layer and reproducibility package as enough for a methodological benchmark. A safer fallback route is *Results in Engineering* or *Energy Reports* if the paper is positioned as an open engineering workflow and benchmark.

Needed strengthening: Keep *Solar Energy* as the first target because the accessible LetPub/New-Ranking search lists it as `2区`, `工程技术`, `能源与燃料`, and SCI/SCIE, and the official Elsevier scope matches solar-energy research, application, and measurement. Do not mention partition data in the manuscript; use it only for submission planning.

## Risk 6: The template and page layout may be questioned

Likely reviewer/author concern: The compact PDF is shorter than the earlier loose draft and may look like a different amount of work.

Current response: The active manuscript uses the official Elsevier `elsarticle` class with `final,3p,times`, which is a compact inspection layout. It removes the earlier excessive spacing and line-numbered review appearance. The content is not reduced; the compact page count is a consequence of using a journal-style format.

Needed strengthening: If Elsevier's submission system requests a review copy, generate a separate `preprint,review,12pt` line-numbered PDF. Keep the compact `final,3p,times` PDF as the visual-quality and internal QA build.

## Risk 7: Computational scale may look hidden

Likely reviewer concern: The reduced PySolTrace matrix may be difficult to interpret without workload and traceability details.

Current response: The revised manuscript states that the all-phase reduced matrix contains 1,485 layout--strategy--condition cases, five layouts, 11 aiming strategies, 27 solar conditions, 6,000 sampled heliostats per layout, and 60,000 requested first-stage ray hits per case. The supplementary table also records `elapsed_s`, with a total recorded case runtime of about 154.46 minutes on the audited run.

Needed strengthening: Keep the workload table in the methods/results bridge and do not imply that this sampled matrix is a full-field annual run.

## Risk 8: Strong baselines may still be considered insufficient

Likely reviewer concern: Low-complexity controls are not enough, and literature methods such as pattern-free, terrain-aware, or collision-analysis-based layouts should be considered.

Current response: The manuscript now includes a same-anchor strong-baseline pressure test with pattern-free-, slider-like-, terrain-aware-, and hybrid-approximation families. These rows preserve the 11,915-coordinate pool and are evaluated with the same SolarPILOT default bridge and aiming proxy. The strongest hybrid row is competitive enough to prevent SOTA-superiority language, while the TS-FPDA and joint queues remain useful as benchmark roles.

Needed strengthening: Do not describe these approximation families as full reproductions of named papers. If a future method-paper route is chosen, implement at least one external optimizer more faithfully and promote the best pressure rows to the same reduced direct/cross-code queue.

## Risk 9: Annual performance and plant-scale realism may still be questioned

Likely reviewer concern: Representative SolarPILOT positions and reduced direct matrices are not the same as an annual plant-performance check.

Current response: The manuscript now includes a fast annual proxy and public plant sanity check. The proxy interpolates SolarPILOT default-aiming optical-efficiency tables over the 8760-hour NASA POWER 2023 weather file, uniformly scales DNI to the reported Dunhuang corrected-TMY annual DNI, and calibrates the baseline thermal proxy against the 351.6 GWh design-generation reference from the public plant paper. This shows that the optical ranking signal is not merely a single-position artifact and that the baseline proxy is in the right plant-scale order.

Needed strengthening: Keep the proxy's boundary explicit. It is not bankable annual yield, SAM dispatch, or full-field annual custom-aimpoint verification. The next hard experiment should still be a same-run annual/custom-aimpoint promotion queue in CoPylot/SolarPILOT, SolTrace, or Solstice.
