# Dunhuang Heliostat Manuscript Journal Target Reassessment

Updated: 2026-06-22 CST, refreshed with live XR Scholar and publisher-page checks

## Short decision

The recommended first target is still **Solar Energy**. It is not the easiest journal, but it is the best topical match for the present manuscript: heliostat-field layout, receiver-flux risk, SolarPILOT/PySolTrace numerical checks, aiming strategy, and reproducible CSP benchmark packaging. The paper should be submitted as a conservative **plant-anchored layout--aiming benchmark and screening workflow**, not as a state-of-the-art optimizer, a certified Dunhuang redesign, or a bankable annual-yield study.

If the authors want a lower-risk fallback, prepare **Sustainable Energy Technologies and Assessments** as route 2 and **International Journal of Green Energy** as route 3. Do not treat **Results in Engineering** or **Energy Reports** as preferred fallbacks unless APC funding is available, because both are fully/gold open-access routes with mandatory APC signals.

## Not preferred as first fallback

- **Results in Engineering**: good engineering metrics and broad scope, but Elsevier identifies it as gold open access. Current official/public signals show a mandatory APC route around USD 2,160. It is not aligned with the no-APC preference.
- **Energy Reports**: broad energy scope, but it is fully open access, with an official Elsevier APC signal around USD 3,220. It is not a no-APC fallback.
- **Renewable Energy**: higher impact and broader renewable-energy audience, but not easier than *Solar Energy*. It would likely require stronger annual performance, techno-economic, or cross-tool validation.
- **Applied Thermal Engineering**: high-quality Elsevier venue with subscription route, but the current manuscript is not primarily a receiver thermal/heat-transfer paper. It would require a stronger receiver thermal-engineering center of gravity.
- **MDPI-style fully OA venues such as Energies**: administratively easy, but inconsistent with the no-APC preference and weaker as a strategic target for this manuscript.

## Solar Energy readiness judgement

The current paper can defensibly try *Solar Energy*, but the cover letter and abstract must keep the evidence boundary strict:

- Say: reproducible Dunhuang-scale layout--aiming benchmark; receiver-flux-aware screening; plant-anchored numerical evidence ladder; reduced direct promotion queue.
- Do not say: SOTA heliostat optimizer; final Dunhuang plant redesign; receiver thermal-safety certification; bankable annual-yield estimate; full commercial retrofit recommendation.

This framing gives the manuscript the best chance because *Solar Energy* readers will understand why full-field preservation, receiver-flux risk, aiming strategy, SolarPILOT/PySolTrace checks, and open reproducibility matter. A more general or lower journal may be easier administratively but can actually be less sympathetic to a specialized heliostat benchmark.
