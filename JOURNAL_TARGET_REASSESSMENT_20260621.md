# Journal Target Reassessment for the Dunhuang Heliostat Manuscript

Updated: 2026-06-21 CST

## Bottom line

The best first target remains *Solar Energy* if the manuscript keeps its current conservative framing: a plant-anchored, receiver-flux-aware layout--aiming benchmark and screening evidence ladder, not a final commercial heliostat-field redesign or state-of-the-art optimizer. The journal is demanding, but it is not an unrealistic target for the current version.

The most credible fallback is *Sustainable Energy Technologies and Assessments* if the authors prefer a broader energy-technology-assessment audience. The safer SCI fallback is *International Journal of Green Energy*, especially if the priority becomes acceptance probability and no mandatory APC rather than Solar/CSP audience fit.

## Recommended submission order

1. *Solar Energy*.
   - Best topic fit for CSP, heliostat fields, SolarPILOT, receiver flux, and aiming strategy.
   - XR Scholar 2026: Engineering / 工程技术 2 区 Top; JCR small category ENERGY & FUELS 3 区.
   - ScienceDirect journal page: CiteScore 12.6, Impact Factor 6.6.
   - Publishing route: hybrid. Open access APC is listed as USD 4,390 excluding taxes, but the subscription route lists no publication fee charged to authors.
   - Risk: medium-high. The manuscript must avoid claiming SOTA optimization or plant-retrofit validity.

2. *Sustainable Energy Technologies and Assessments*.
   - Best fallback if the paper is positioned as a benchmark, screening workflow, and energy-technology assessment.
   - XR Scholar 2026: Engineering / 工程技术 2 区 Top; JCR GREEN & SUSTAINABLE SCIENCE & TECHNOLOGY 3 区 and ENERGY & FUELS 3 区.
   - ScienceDirect journal page: CiteScore 17.3, Impact Factor 7.0.
   - Publishing route: Elsevier hybrid-style route should be reconfirmed in the submission system before upload.
   - Risk: medium. Broader audience, but less specifically CSP/heliostat-oriented than *Solar Energy*.

3. *International Journal of Green Energy*.
   - Practical SCI fallback with lower prestige but better tolerance for applied renewable-energy workflows.
   - XR Scholar 2026: Engineering / 工程技术 3 区; JCR THERMODYNAMICS 3 区, GREEN & SUSTAINABLE SCIENCE & TECHNOLOGY 4 区, ENERGY & FUELS 4 区.
   - Taylor & Francis page: 2024 Impact Factor 3.1; best JCR quartile Q2; hybrid Open Select.
   - Publishing route: Taylor & Francis states that if authors do not choose open access, there is no APC.
   - Risk: lower. The fit is broader and less CSP-specialized.

4. ASME *Journal of Solar Energy Engineering*.
   - Strong solar-engineering scope but not aligned with the user's Q2/Q3 target in the current XR Scholar view.
   - XR Scholar 2026: Engineering / 工程技术 4 区; JCR ENERGY & FUELS 4 区 and ENGINEERING, MECHANICAL 4 区.
   - ASME page: Impact Factor 1.9.
   - Publishing route: ASME states there are no fees to submit and publish a standard research paper; OA is optional.
   - Use only as a safety fallback if the priority becomes field-fit plus no mandatory publication fee.

## Not recommended as first targets

- *Renewable Energy*: very strong journal and XR Scholar Engineering 1 区 Top. It is probably harder than *Solar Energy* for the current manuscript unless annual techno-economic and receiver-thermal validation are strengthened.
- *Energy*: broader and higher-bar; not a good match for a heliostat benchmark without deeper system/thermal/economic proof.
- *Applied Thermal Engineering*: XR/JCR metrics are attractive, but the manuscript currently does not contain enough receiver thermal-stress or heat-transfer modelling to make this the natural first home.
- *Journal of Energy Storage*: metrics are attractive but the scope is not aligned with a heliostat-field layout benchmark.
- *Journal of Renewable and Sustainable Energy*: safe but too low in the current XR Scholar view relative to the user's desired Q2/Q3 path.

## Practical recommendation

Submit first to *Solar Energy* using the existing Elsevier `elsarticle` package. Keep *Sustainable Energy Technologies and Assessments* as the prepared transfer target. If the user and supervisor want a more conservative SCI path after one rejection, retitle and reframe for *International Journal of Green Energy* rather than immediately dropping to a lower-rank solar-engineering venue.

The cover letter should explicitly state that the manuscript contributes a reproducible Dunhuang full-field layout--aiming benchmark, not a certified retrofit design. This reduces reviewer attack surface and makes *Solar Energy* more realistic.
