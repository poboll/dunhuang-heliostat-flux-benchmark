# Journal Target Reassessment for the Dunhuang Heliostat Manuscript

Updated: 2026-06-22 CST

## Executive recommendation

The best first target remains *Solar Energy*. It is demanding, but it is still the most coherent first submission target for the current manuscript because the paper is about a plant-anchored heliostat-field layout--aiming benchmark, SolarPILOT/PySolTrace verification, receiver-flux risk, and solar-tower engineering practice. The manuscript should be submitted as a conservative benchmark and screening-evidence paper, not as a certified commercial plant redesign or state-of-the-art optimizer.

The best prepared fallback is *Sustainable Energy Technologies and Assessments* if the authors want a broader energy-technology-assessment readership. The safer acceptance-oriented SCI fallback is *International Journal of Green Energy*, especially if the priority becomes Q3/SCI stability and a no-mandatory-APC publishing route.

## Journals not recommended as the first target

- *Renewable Energy*: ranking and metrics are stronger than *Solar Energy*, not safer. XR Scholar lists Engineering 1 区 Top and ENERGY & FUELS 2 区; Elsevier reports higher impact metrics. For the current manuscript it would likely require stronger annual techno-economic, receiver-thermal, or cross-code validation.
- *Applied Thermal Engineering*: attractive metrics, but the manuscript is not primarily about receiver heat transfer or thermal-stress modeling.
- *Energy Conversion and Management: X*: strong metrics, but it is open-access/mirror-journal oriented and conflicts with the no-mandatory-APC preference.
- *Journal of Renewable and Sustainable Energy*: viable but too low in XR Scholar for the user's stated Q2/Q3 target.
- MDPI-style OA venues such as *Energies*: easier administratively but poor fit with the no-APC preference and weaker prestige strategy.

## Can the current paper reach Solar Energy?

Yes, but only if submitted with the current conservative framing. The realistic acceptance path is not "we invented the best heliostat optimizer"; it is "we provide a reproducible Dunhuang full-field layout--aiming benchmark with receiver-flux-aware screening and direct-promotion evidence." The paper's main strengths are plant anchoring, full-field preservation, receiver-flux risk visibility, multiple numerical gates, and reproducible packaging. Its main vulnerability is that it is not a full annual bankable redesign and does not yet have full-field custom-aimpoint/cross-code annual validation.

Therefore *Solar Energy* is high but defensible. It is a better first target than immediately downgrading because it is the cleanest audience match and has a no-mandatory-publication-fee subscription route. If rejected for insufficient validation depth, the prepared fallback should be *Sustainable Energy Technologies and Assessments* or *International Journal of Green Energy*, not a random low-rank solar journal.
